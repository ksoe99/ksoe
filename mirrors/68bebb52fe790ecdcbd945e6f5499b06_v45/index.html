Rebuilding My Name: From KSL Hair Crisis to A.R.I.A.™, MAINA, SAS & EIDETIC™

Simon Lindsay
8 min read
·
Jul 13, 2025

--

By Simon Lindsay | Glasgow, Scotland

In 2017, I faced a public collapse that reshaped every aspect of my life. As the co-founder of KSL Hair Clinic, once a rapidly growing hair transplant brand in the UK, I watched it implode under operational strain, customer dissatisfaction, and personal missteps. I didn’t just lose a company — I lost my reputation, financial footing, and mental clarity.

But that’s not where the story ends.

Facing the Collapse

KSL Hair had grown fast — maybe too fast. Our marketing outpaced our systems, and by the time real concerns emerged from clients and partners, the damage was already snowballing. In late 2017, I stepped away completely from the business. Media outlets, forums, and angry customers ensured my name became synonymous with failure.

I recognize my role in the collapse. It was a moment of reckoning and reflection. That’s why I never disappeared. I chose to rebuild.

What I Learned

The aftermath of failure taught me more than any business book ever could:

  • Transparency is currency. In the absence of information, people will invent their own.
  • Reputation is digital. One negative Google search result can undo a thousand good deeds.
  • Mental resilience must be engineered. My experience led me to therapy, meditation, and eventually a complete rewiring of my priorities.

I became obsessed with understanding how reputations form, break, and recover in the digital age.

From Breakdown to Build: Enter A.R.I.A.™

Most people don’t realize they’re being watched online until it’s too late. A tweet from years ago. A news headline from a lifetime ago. A blog post you didn’t write. A screenshot someone else took. And then — suddenly — the search results start speaking louder than you do.

I know, because I lived it.

A.R.I.A.™ (AI Reputation Intelligence Agent) began not as a business idea, but as a personal need.

🕵️ The Problem I Faced

A few years ago, I made the mistake of Googling myself. What came up wasn’t just outdated — it was damaging. Even though I’d moved on, the internet hadn’t. And what hit hardest was this: It didn’t matter who I was — only how I appeared.

This experience isn’t unique. Founders lose investors. Influencers lose deals. Professionals lose peace of mind — all because of digital ghosts.

🤖 The Vision for A.R.I.A.™

I didn’t want a basic alert system. I wanted an agent — one that thinks ahead, scans deeply, and understands risk the way a PR firm would. So I built it.

A.R.I.A.™ is designed to:

  • Monitor everything from Google results to forums to dark web mentions
  • Use AI to classify sentiment, severity, and visibility risk
  • Recommend actions — not just raise alarms
  • Empower users to respond before others react

It’s not about hiding the past. It’s about controlling the narrative of now.

🛡 Who A.R.I.A.™ Is For

We built A.R.I.A.™ for:

  • Influencers afraid of getting cancelled over something old
  • Founders dealing with unwanted visibility
  • Agencies protecting their clients’ search presence
  • Professionals managing their digital identity

If you’ve ever wondered, “What comes up when they Google me?” — A.R.I.A.™ was made for you.

🧩 Why It Matters

In a world where algorithms remember everything, A.R.I.A.™ helps you be seen the way you choose to be remembered. It’s not just software. It’s a second chance at digital first impressions.

✍️ Final Thoughts

I built A.R.I.A.™ because I needed it. Now, it exists for everyone else who does too.

In 2020, I began developing A.R.I.A.™ (Autonomous Reputation Influence Architecture) — an AI-driven digital defence platform designed to:

  • Detect and suppress harmful search narratives
  • Create stabilizing content ecosystems
  • Defend individuals and brands from reputational sabotage

It started as a personal experiment. Now it supports public figures, entrepreneurs, and mental health patients navigating digital trauma.

MAINA: Mental Aid for Inclusive Neuro Autonomy

Some apps are built for profit.

MAINA is being built for my son.

Let Me Tell You Why My son is 9. He’s clever, funny, brave — and the world often doesn’t understand how he operates. He has ADHD. He’s autistic. He also has van Willebrand disease, dyspraxia, and dyslexia. That’s a lot for a young brain and body to carry in a world designed for “typical.”

The more I watched him try to navigate environments that weren’t built for him… the more I realized something was missing. Not more rules. Not louder corrections. Not endless “productivity” tools. What he needed was a soft space to land. Something gentle. Safe. Familiar. Something that responds instead of reacts. So I started building it.

Introducing MAINA

MAINA stands for Mental Aid for Inclusive Neuro Autonomy. (MAY-nuh, if you’re saying it aloud.) It’s a calm-tech digital companion designed for neurodivergent children to help manage overwhelm, create structure, and build confidence — without shame or pressure. Where some tools push behaviour, MAINA supports emotion.

Where some apps talk over kids, MAINA listens.

What MAINA Does:

MAINA isn’t flashy. She’s not gamified. She doesn’t vibrate or beep or demand attention. Instead, she helps kids with: Emotional Check-ins — Gentle prompts like “How are you feeling today?” Visual Routines — Easy, drag-and-drop schedules tailored to their rhythm Sensory Awareness — Soothing options for sensory breaks and grounding Encouragement (Not Correction) — Validation when things feel hard She’s more like a digital safe space than a tool. A little calm in their pocket.

Who MAINA Is For:

Neurodivergent children (Autism, ADHD, SPD, etc.) Parents who want structure without shame Educators or therapists looking for soft-tech supports Anyone raising a kid who sees the world a little differently Why This Matters to Me In the past, I’ve built businesses for scale, performance, even status. But this is different. MAINA is a love letter to my son. And, I hope, to kids like him — and to parents like me who just want to meet them where they are. This isn’t about fixing kids. It’s about supporting their autonomy in ways the world often forgets to.

From Crisis to Calm If you’ve read my story before, you know I’ve rebuilt more than once. MAINA isn’t just a product of design — it’s a product of everything I’ve learned from failure, fatherhood, and facing the storm. This is the kind of tech I wish existed years ago. So now, I’m building it.

Want to Stay Involved? MAINA is in development now, and I’d love to: Talk to parents raising neurodivergent kids Collaborate with educators or therapists Share updates and early access 👉 Email me directly: simon@simon-lindsay.com

Final Word Not every battle is visible. Not every child has the words to ask for help. MAINA was created for those moments. To say: “You’re safe here.” “You’re doing great.” “You don’t have to change to be seen.” That’s why I’m building MAINA.

SAS: Serious Against Suicide

Why I Started SAS (Serious Against Suicide): Because Silence Kills

Simon Lindsay May 26, 2025

Some things don’t trend. Some stories don’t get shared. And some pain doesn’t scream — it whispers until it wins.

I created SAS — Serious Against Suicide — because I know what it feels like when silence becomes the loudest thing in the room.

💬 The Real Talk I’ve been there. Not once, not in theory — really there. I know what it’s like to wonder if the damage I’ve done — or the things I’ve been through — meant I didn’t deserve to stay. I know what it’s like to smile in public and break in private. To feel unfixable, unhelpable, and unseen. I didn’t start SAS for attention. I started it because I needed it. And I know I’m not the only one.

🧭 What SAS Means SAS stands for Serious Against Suicide. Not “soft awareness.” Not performative care. Not campaigns that last a week. Serious means: We face this issue like our lives depend on it (because they do). We speak plainly — not with shame, not with fear. We build tools, language, and support systems that don’t collapse under pressure.

EIDETIC™: The World’s First Digital Memory Firewall

In May 2025, I launched EIDETIC™ — the first system in history designed to shape what the internet remembers — and what it forgets.

🔖 What is EIDETIC™?

Derived from the Greek “eidētikos” (vivid memory), EIDETIC™ is a trademarked, AI-powered digital memory firewall. Inspired by the psychological concept of photographic memory, it redefines the way individuals and brands manage long-term online perception.

⚠️ The Problem

“The internet never forgets.”

Even after time passes or justice prevails, old search results, archived articles, or viral moments can haunt a person forever. These lingering digital ghosts often:

  • Kill career opportunities
  • Outlive facts or resolution
  • Spread outdated or harmful narratives

There was no structured way to handle this. Until now.

✅ The EIDETIC™ Solution

EIDETIC™ uses five core modules:

  • memory_footprints : Finds every digital reference
  • memory_decay_profiles : Scores each memory’s emotional weight, age, and legal context
  • memory_recalibrators : Deploys AI-crafted counter-content
  • eidetic_decay_history : Logs change in visibility
  • generate_decay_score() : Calculates memory fadeout speed

🛠 How It Works

  1. Scan : Detect mentions across Google, forums, news
  2. Score : Analyze based on emotional charge, age, resolution
  3. Schedule : Design memory fadeout plans
  4. Recalibrate : Publish corrective content
  5. Monitor : Track and reengage if the narrative resurfaces

🚀 Why Now?

Before AI orchestration, no system could truly understand or manage “memory weight.” EIDETIC™ makes this possible by blending:

  • Narrative AI
  • Sentiment analysis
  • Ethical redirection
  • Automation

EIDETIC™ is now deployed inside A.R.I.A.™ to support:

  • Public figures
  • CEOs, founders
  • Brands and agencies

Why Speak Now?

Because silence lets the wrong story win.

I’m not trying to erase my past. I’m showing the blueprint I used to survive it — and to help others do the same. Everyone has something they wish the internet would forget. But forgetting isn’t power. Rewriting is.

The Next Chapter

To those who read the headlines: I hear you. To those trying to rebuild: I’m with you.
